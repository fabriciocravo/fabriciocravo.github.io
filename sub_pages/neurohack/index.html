FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who should attend NeuroHack Boston 2025?

This workshop is designed for undergraduate students, graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, faculty members, and industry professionals working in neuroscience or related fields. Whether you're just starting your research journey or are an established scientist, you'll find valuable opportunities to learn cutting-edge techniques, share your work, and build meaningful connections within Boston's vibrant neuroscience community.

Will meals be provided?

Yes! We'll provide breakfast to start your day, a nutritious lunch, and coffee breaks throughout the workshop to keep you energized. All dietary restrictions and preferences will be accommodated. Please indicate any special requirements during registration.

What's the workshop format?

NeuroHack Boston features a dynamic mix of keynote presentations by leading researchers, hands-on technical workshops, interactive discussion panels, and dedicated networking sessions. You'll have opportunities to present your own work, collaborate on new projects, and engage in both formal presentations and informal conversations with peers and mentors from across Boston's research institutions.
